Disequilibrium room

by Esther Zhang2021

Disequilibrium room is a multi-sensory interactive installation allowing people to experience the subtle relationship between sound and body balance.

Highlights

  • academic research
  • education
  • physical installation
  • sound design

Background story

With a background in electrical computer and engineering, I am always excited about how science and engineering could give answers for unanswered quetions and help me better understand myself and the world around me instead of just accepting the way it is. Thus, I wish to demonstrate either the groundbreaking discoveries or the omnipresent phenomenon with sound academic pedestals to the general public with more straightforward language and engaging experience. Based on the research, we designed a sound and visual interactive space to demonstrate the how impaired hearing could affect the body balance.

Abstract

The project is based upon assistant professor of physical therapyphysical therapy Dr. Anat Lubetzky and vice chair, music associate professor of music technology music and performing arts professions Prof. Agnieszka Roginska's research research in the simulation and applications of immersive and 3D audio including the capture, analysis and synthesis of auditory environments, auditory displays and its applications in augmented acoustic sensing.

Research/Context

Dr. Anat and Prof. Agnieszka Roginska's research in utilizing advances in virtual reality technology to study multi-sensory integration for postural control in adults with and without vestibular dysfunction and hearing loss across the life span.
